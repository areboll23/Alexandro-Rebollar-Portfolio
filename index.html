FEA · Structural Analysis · Standards Application
Trail King Outrigger Mount — Structural Failure Analysis
✓ Root Cause Identified & Resolved
The Challenge

A lower outrigger mount on a Trail King trailer bed was experiencing catastrophic structural failure under standard operational loads — described internally as "blowing to bits." The failure mode was recurring and needed a definitive root-cause diagnosis, not a patch.

My Role

Lead Design Analyst. Owned the full investigation: simulation setup, standards research, material library creation, and final redesign recommendation.

Approach

Ran high-fidelity FEA in SolidWorks to map stress concentrations under the operational load case. Researched ASME/SAE standards for bolt-hole edge distance and minimum plate thickness. Built a custom material library to ensure the simulation matched Trail King's specific steel grade — not a generic approximation.

Result

Identified two critical flaws: insufficient plate thickness and a sub-standard edge-to-hole (tear-out) distance. Delivered a redesigned geometry meeting industry standards that eliminated the failure mode entirely.

Key Engineering Principle Applied

The primary failure was bolt-hole tear-out — a shear failure that occurs when the edge distance from bolt center to plate edge is insufficient. Industry standard requires a minimum edge distance of 1.5× to 2× the bolt diameter (1.5D–2D). The original geometry fell below this threshold, concentrating shear stress at the plate edge under lateral outrigger loads and initiating progressive tearing.
